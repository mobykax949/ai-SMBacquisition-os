SECTION 12

Three lanes. One funnel.

Where deals actually come from, in priority order.

Lane 1 · Pre-market

Brokers & pocket listings

The premium deals sell quietly to known, financed buyers before they list. The broker-finder gets you on that call sheet.

Lane 2 · Off-market

The owner who hasn't listed

Best margin, most work. The deal-finder surfaces the succession-age owner running an analog business — the strongest single signal is a great business with no website.

Lane 3 · On-market

The safety net

Web search monitors listings inside your buy box so a good deal never slips past. Weighted last, never the main hunt.

SECTION 14

The week-one playbook.

Seven days from zero to a ranked target list and your first outreach staged. Do these in order.

DAY 1

Set up the workspace. Claude Pro, desktop app, one Project named for your acquisition initiative. Load guides 01–02.

DAY 2

Build your buy box. Run the buy-box builder. Three lanes, scored verticals, saved to the Project. This is the engine's ground truth.

DAY 3

First sourcing run. Run the deal finder on your #1 vertical. Expect 10–20 real businesses; flag the no-website operators.

DAY 4

Qualify the top five. AQ-score them. Kill fast — the 70% Rule says most should die. Two or three survive.

DAY 5

Find three brokers. Run the broker finder for your vertical + geography. Draft the buyer-intro outreach; you want pocket listings flowing.

DAY 6

Stage your first outreach. Outreach engine, related-business angle, your two best targets. Review every word before it moves.

DAY 7

Build the dashboard. One HTML file, your whole pipeline. Then rest — the engine doesn't need you to scroll listings anymore.

SECTION 15

216 ways to fund the deal.

The single biggest myth in acquisitions is that you need the purchase price in cash. The method this system is built on catalogs 216 distinct funding strategies — and real deals stack several at once to drive cash-at-closing toward zero.

216
Funding strategies
13
Strategy families
3+
Stacked per deal
~$0
Cash at closing, done right
FAMILY A–C

Seller-side structures

Owner-carry notes, holdbacks, earnouts, deferred down payments, and consulting or IP agreements that offset the purchase price with the seller's own future involvement.

FAMILY D–G

Asset & debt plays

Asset carveouts, sale-leasebacks, inventory consignment, debt assumption and restructuring — funding the buy from what the business already owns and owes.

FAMILY H–M

Third-party capital & cashflow

SBA structures, co-investors, revenue-based financing, vendor financing, pre-sale promotions that generate real cash from the target's own customer list before you fund the buy.

SECTION 21

Appendix: troubleshooting & care.

Claude forgot my buy box mid-conversation
Long chats compact and lose their middle. The fix is structural: your buy box lives in the Project, not the chat. If it's only in chat history, save it to the Project now and reference it by name. Rule: files survive, chat memory doesn't.
My skill runs differently than the demo
Check the model picker first — build in the model you run. A skill authored against the top-tier model behaves differently on the fast tier. Match the model named in the skill's header, then re-run.
I'm hitting usage limits mid-analysis
Big runs (a 20-target pull, a 13-criterion score) are token-heavy. Run them early in your session, not after hours of chat. Save outputs to files immediately so a re-run is never needed. If limits bite weekly, that's the signal to move up a plan tier.
The deal finder returned junk categories
Category bleed is real — "hood cleaning" pulls pressure washers. Add the category-match line from the skill's pitfalls section: only include businesses whose primary service is [X]; list excluded near-misses separately.
Can outreach send without me?
No — by design. Every message the engine drafts stages for your review. You are the send button. An acquisition is a relationship; the AI does the volume, you do the trust.
Where do downloads live?
Everything ships from the repo: /skills (drop into Claude Desktop), /prompts (the vault), /setup (guides 01–08). Star it — updates land there first.
