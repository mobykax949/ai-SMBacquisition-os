Lesson 08b — The CFE Converter

The owner won't sell? Don't hang up. Turn "not ready" into permanent equity with consulting-for-equity.

Reading time: ~8 min · Track: Acquire (fork) · Prerequisite: an owner who said "not ready to sell" (outreach-engine Lesson 08, or discovery-interviewer Lesson 10)

What this does

In the EPIC method every owner you reach lands in one of three outcomes, and none of them is a dead end: acquire (they want to sell), agency client / CFE (not ready to sell but the business is real and needs help), or referral (not a fit, but they know someone). This skill runs the middle path — consulting-for-equity. When an owner won't sell but has obvious untapped upside, you pitch advisory work in exchange for permanent equity instead of a fee, structured on Roland Frasier's default 10/10/10 model. You become "the other eye" that sees the money already in front of them, then take a stake for unlocking it. It is how you make a "no" pay — no outreach is wasted.

You'll walk away with: cfe-<business>-YYYY-MM-DD.md — the upside levers, a consulting scope, a 10/10/10 equity structure with a vesting trigger, the tax path flagged, and a staged partnership pitch.

Step 2 — Give Claude what it needs

  • An owner who is not selling but runs a real, profitable business with visible upside they aren't capturing. (If they want to sell, this is the wrong skill — go to aq-analyzer.)
  • Your honest read of what they're leaving on the table: no marketing system, manual operations, no backend offer, no acquisition or exit plan.
  • What you bring: skills, systems, network, AI-operations capability, acquisitions know-how.
  • Their rough numbers if you have them (current profit, historical baseline) — the equity vests off that baseline.

Step 3 — Run it (copy this)

/cfe-converter

Or frame the case directly:

Run the cfe-converter skill. Owner won't sell but has a real business: [name, industry, size, what they do]. What they're not capturing: [untapped upside]. What I bring: [my skills/systems/network]. Be the other eye — name the top value levers, draft a consulting scope, and structure a 10/10/10 equity deal (greater of 10% of profits or $10k/month, plus 10% of exit), performance-vested against their baseline. Flag the tax question for their CPA.

What Claude creates

File / output What it is
"The other eye" levers The 1–3 highest-value things this owner can't see because they're focused on the thing they sell — each with the transformation named
Consulting deliverables scope What you'd advise on: business model, value ladder, marketing, growth, acquisitions, exit — and how you contribute
10/10/10 equity structure Greater of 10% of profits OR $10k/month, plus 10% of the exit price — permanent, at holdco level where one exists, performance-vested against a baseline
Tax path (flagged) Profits-only interest, or a new co both parties contribute into — flagged for their CPA, not tax advice
Partnership pitch Under 200 words, framed as "I only win when you win," staged for your approval

How it works: owners undervalue themselves because they're caught up in the thing they sell and can't see the transformation ladder worth far more. You supply the outside view and take equity for unlocking it. The equity vests off a measured baseline ("as long as we beat X, our stake vests") so you're paid on outperformance — and so you don't inflate the valuation against yourself before you've locked your piece. And it usually stacks: equity on top of your normal fee, not instead of it.

Step 4 — Test it, refine it, stack it

Test (pressure-test the pitch):

Read my CFE pitch as the skeptical owner. Where would I balk at giving up equity? Rewrite it so the value I unlock clearly dwarfs the equity I'm asking for, and add a JV bridge option if they still resist.

Refine (tune the structure):

Compute the baseline from their last [N] periods: [numbers]. Set the vesting trigger off it, and show me the deal if I take equity in BOTH the core business and its monetization layer.

Stack it: a CFE client is a warm future acquisition — and every "no" still has a third path.

This owner became a CFE client. Log them, and if they weren't a fit at all, run the outreach-engine referral path: draft the ask for who else they know in [vertical].

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Take permanent equity, not a one-time JV. A JV is only a bridge to prove credibility when the owner won't grant equity yet. The goal is a permanent stake in the company — don't settle short.
  • Don't raise the valuation before you take equity. Inflate the number first and the owner needs you less and offers a smaller piece. Vest off a baseline; lock your stake before you drive the value up.
  • Stack equity on top of your fee. The move is usually both, not either/or. Leaving the fee behind is as much a miss as leaving the equity.
  • Never hand-wave the tax question. Equity in an existing valuable company can be taxable on receipt. Profits-only interest or a fresh co — and send it to their CPA.
  • Sell the transformation, not the thing. If your pitch is about their product or service instead of the money they're leaving on the table, you've missed the whole point of being the other eye.

Where this fits

A fork off the outreach funnel (after Lesson 08), not a step in it. This is the second lane of the Magic Intersection — acquire · CFE/agency · referral — the reason no outreach is wasted. Owners who say "not now" to selling become equity stakes or, later, warm acquisition leads you already understand from the inside. From here you rejoin the funnel at Lesson 09 — The AQ Analyzer for the owners who do want to sell.
