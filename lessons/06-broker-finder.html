Lesson 06 — The Broker Finder

Get on broker lists as a credible, financed buyer — and see pocket listings before they go public.

Reading time: ~8 min · Track: Acquire · Prerequisite: buy-box-builder (Lesson 03)

What this does

Brokers are Frasier's recommended channel for getting your reps in — and the gold lane for pre-market deal flow. This skill finds and profiles the brokers who actually work your geography and verticals, ranks the best fits, and drafts the credible buyer introduction that separates you from the fifty tire-kicker emails a broker gets each week: who you are, exactly what you're hunting (buy box attached), and proof of financing (SBA pre-qualification, 1031 equity, or cash). Serious plus financed plus specific is what earns pocket listings.

You'll walk away with: a ranked list of 5 target brokers, an under-200-word intro email with your buy box attached, and a relationship tracker.

Step 1 — Install the plugin (one time, gets you every skill)

All skills ship in the SMB Wealth Builder plugin. (Already installed? Skip to Step 2.)

  1. In Claude Desktop: Customize → Plugins tab.
  2. + → Add marketplace → paste mobykax949/ai-SMBacquisition-os
  3. Install smb-wealth-builder, User scope.
  4. New Cowork chat, type / — the skills appear.

Done when: /broker-finder shows in a fresh Cowork chat.

Step 2 — Give Claude what it needs

  • buy-box.md in Project knowledge, plus a 1-page PDF version to attach to emails (ask Claude to format your buy box as a one-pager, then print/save it to PDF).
  • Proof of financing to reference: an SBA pre-qualification letter, 1031 equity amount parked with a qualified intermediary, or cash available. If you have none of these yet, get financing lined up before running this — brokers will ask on the first call.
  • Your target geography and top 2 verticals (brokers specialize; narrow the search).

Step 3 — Run it (copy this)

/broker-finder

Or scope it:

Run the broker-finder skill. Geography: [area]. Verticals: [top 2]. Deal size: [$1-5M revenue, $200K+ SDE]. Financing: [SBA 7(a) pre-qualified / $X of 1031 equity / cash]. Find and profile brokers, rank the top 5 by fit, and draft my buyer introduction email — under 200 words, buy box attached.

What Claude creates

File / output What it is
Broker research table Broker, firm, contact, specialization, recent deals — pulled from web search and directories
Ranked top 5 The best fits for your size, verticals, and financing style, with a 1–2 sentence why for each
Buyer introduction email Under 200 words: who you are, what you hunt, proof of financing in the first 3 sentences, pocket-listing ask, 15-minute call CTA
Relationship tracker Broker, firm, intro-call date, deals shared, last follow-up, next follow-up due

How it works: brokers want buyers who close. The email proves three things fast — you're specific (buy box attached), you're financed (named in the opening), and you're decisive (you want off-market looks and a short call). After the intro call, the pocket-listing question is: "What deals are you working on right now that match my criteria, even if they're not listed yet?" Then it's a cadence game: a short check-in every 2–4 weeks keeps you top-of-mind when the right deal walks in.

Step 4 — Test it, refine it, stack it

Test (pressure-check the email):

Read my broker intro email as a skeptical broker who gets 50 of these a week. What would make you ignore it? Tighten it: financing proof in the first 3 sentences, one specific ask, under 200 words.

Refine (no responses after a week):

I sent the intro to 5 brokers a week ago with no responses. Diagnose: is the email the problem or the broker selection? Draft one revised email and pick 5 different brokers to try.

Stack it: when a pocket listing arrives, qualify it fast.

A broker sent me this listing: [paste details]. Run the aq-analyzer skill on it today — brokers remember buyers who respond within 24 hours.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Never send a generic email with no buy box. Without specific criteria and financing proof you look like a tire-kicker, and brokers filter tire-kickers for a living.
  • Don't contact brokers before financing is real. "Are you pre-qualified?" is the first question on the intro call. If the answer is no, they won't send you deals — line up financing first.
  • Don't blast 50 brokers at once. Brokers in the same market talk. Start with 5, refine, then expand.
  • The intro call is the beginning, not the close. Check in every 2–4 weeks even when you're not ready to offer. Silent buyers get forgotten; consistent ones get the first look.
  • Never ghost a broker after they send a deal. Respond within 48 hours even to pass — with the reason. "Doesn't fit because [X], keep me in mind for [vertical]" keeps the pipeline alive.

Where this fits

The goal is 3–5 active broker relationships feeding you pocket listings monthly. Next: Lesson 07 — Market Monitor to cover the on-market safety net.
