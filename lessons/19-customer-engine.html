Lesson 19 — The Customer Engine

Monetize the customer list you already own: reviews, win-backs, referrals, and follow-up that actually happens.

Reading time: ~8 min · Track: Modernize · Prerequisite: operations stabilized, ~5 SOPs documented (Lessons 15–17)

What this does

Most small businesses have revenue sitting dormant in their customer list: past customers gone quiet, happy customers never asked to refer, completed jobs with no review request, and follow-ups that live in the owner's head and never happen consistently. This skill builds four systems that run in the background — a review-generation cadence (request 3–7 days after job completion), a 3-email win-back campaign for dormant customers, a referral system with an easy form and an incentive, and five reusable AI-drafted nurture templates. The same rule as the modernization audit applies: nothing touches active customer relationships in week one — you build and test on past and future customers first.

You'll walk away with: four deployable systems — review sequence, win-back campaign, referral ask + form, and five nurture templates — with 30-day targets to measure against.

Step 2 — Give Claude what it needs

  • Your customer list in one place: names, emails, last purchase date, lifetime spend. Scattered across QuickBooks, spreadsheets, and email? Consolidate first.
  • Segments counted: active (bought in 90 days), recent (3–6 months), dormant (6+ months), one-time-never-returned.
  • An email tool or CRM to run the automations (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or even manual sends to start).
  • Works best with 100+ past customers and a repeat purchase cycle.

What Claude creates

File / output What it is
Review sequence (2 emails) Thank-you + review link at day 3; gentle reminder at day 10, with a private-feedback path for unhappy customers
Win-back campaign (3 emails) "We miss you" with offer → value-add tip or case study → last-chance with a time-limited incentive and a clean unsubscribe
Referral system Warm request email to your happiest customers plus the referral form questions (name, contact, service needed)
5 nurture templates Quote follow-up, post-purchase check-in, seasonal reminder, educational value-add, soft upsell/cross-sell — each under 100 words, reusable

How it works: each system monetizes trust you already earned. Reviews compound (10 new reviews move your local ranking, which moves inbound calls); dormant customers convert at a fraction of new-customer acquisition cost because they already know you; a small referral incentive (say $25) can return a customer worth many times that in lifetime value. The 30-day targets keep it honest: 10+ new reviews, 5–10% win-back response, 3+ referrals, 10–20% nurture reply rate.

Step 4 — Test it, refine it, stack it

Test (segment safety check):

Before anything sends: verify my send lists. Show me who would receive the win-back campaign and confirm no one who purchased in the last 90 days is on it.

Refine (day 30 numbers):

30-day results: [reviews posted, win-back responses, referrals, nurture replies]. Compare to targets, diagnose the weakest system, and revise its messaging. Should we add SMS to the review requests?

Stack it: recurring revenue moves the multiple.

These systems added [X] reactivated customers and [Y]% more repeat purchases. Re-score my recurring-revenue dimension in the multiple-builder skill.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Never send win-back emails to active customers. Blasting "we miss you" at someone who bought last week looks disorganized and burns trust. Segment carefully; actives get different messaging.
  • Time the review ask. Same-day requests land before the customer experienced the result. Wait 3–7 days — 2 weeks for slow-outcome services like landscaping or pest control.
  • Check the inbox daily. Automated sequences plus ignored replies equals missed revenue. A customer who replies with a question gets a personal answer, not another automation.
  • Offer the referral incentive. Some customers refer for free; most need the nudge. The $25 gift card ROI is not a close call.
  • A 3% response is a system working, not failing. Refine the offer, run it again in 90 days. The customer whose situation changed since last time responds next time.

Where this fits
